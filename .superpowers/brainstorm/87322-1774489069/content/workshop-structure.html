How should the workshop be structured?

Each option gets students to a working app — they differ in how much they build vs. receive

A

Build Together Live

Students start from the template repo and follow you milestone by milestone. You build on screen, they build alongside. Cover milestones 1–3 live, then merge a pre-built branch for the rest so everyone finishes with a working app.

Pros

  • Most authentic to your real workflow
  • Students feel the "aha" moments
  • Shows Claude Code in action

Cons

  • Pace varies — fast students wait, slow students fall behind
  • Live coding can hit surprises
B

You Demo, They Catch Up

You live-code each milestone on screen while explaining your thought process. After each milestone, students run a git checkpoint branch to sync up. Everyone finishes together regardless of pace.

Pros

  • No one falls behind permanently
  • Controlled pacing
  • Students still build

Cons

  • Less organic — feels more scripted
  • Students may not internalize the workflow
C

Concepts First, Then Build Sprint

First 30 min: you walk through superpowers, your mental model, and show the finished app. Last 60–90 min: students build from the template at their own pace with you floating to help. Pre-built branches as checkpoints.

Pros

  • Students understand the "why" before the "how"
  • More workshop-like feel
  • Flexible pacing

Cons

  • Less collaborative building energy
  • Some may finish less than others
